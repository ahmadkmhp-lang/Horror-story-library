## PHASE 3 — STRUCTURE BREAKDOWN (12-beat model → 3 templates)

- **T-A "Cold-Open Death → Rewind"** (01, 03, 07, 16, 24, 28, 30): Opening Hook = the ending shown → flashback Normal World → … → Climax loops back to the open. Strongest retention engine.
- **T-B "Arrival / Move-in Haunt"** (02, 06, 17, 25, 26): Normal World → Trigger (new house/room) → Warning → escalating events → Investigation → Reveal/Twist → flee/seal.
- **T-C "Rule / Bargain / Road"** (05, 09, 18, 22, 23, 29, 31, 32): Established Rule or Bargain → Trigger (break/accept) → Warning ignored → Disturbance → Escalation → Reveal of true cost → Climax → Aftertaste (cycle/warning).

**Universal beat presence:** Opening Hook 32/32 · Warning Stage 29/32 (near-mandatory) · First Paranormal Event ≈ 30–40% mark · Revelation+Twist fused in T-A · Final Aftertaste/"it still happens" 28/32.

## PHASE 5 — RETENTION ENGINE

Recurring devices & why they hold viewers:
- **Cold-open ending** (T-A): primary curiosity gap "how does it get here?"
- **The Rule** ("don't go out after 7," "don't look at the river," "don't look back"): an open loop the viewer waits to see broken.
- **Recurring landmark / frozen clock (3:33):** escalating "we're trapped" dread (07,29,30,31,32).
- **Object that returns after destruction** (15,22): defeats false relief, re-opens the loop.
- **Delayed answer** ("what happened on the trip / 12 yrs ago / who is she") held to ~80%.
- **Final-line twist / "it still happens":** retention past the end (subscribe/share).
- **Frame devices** (podcast 10, true-footage 31, asylum confession 24): authenticity hook.
**Why they keep watching:** one dominant unanswered question planted early, withheld, paid with a reversal — then a smaller loop re-opened at the very end.
